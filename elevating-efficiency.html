Elevating Efficiency Beyond Short Sprints

By Dirk Steele

Read on LinkedIn

Sprint velocity is one of the most commonly tracked metrics in agile delivery — and one of the most frequently misread. High velocity does not guarantee high value, and teams that optimize for points completed often find themselves moving fast toward the wrong outcomes.

This article argues that true efficiency requires a broader lens: one that accounts for throughput stability, quality signals, and the health of the work entering the sprint, not just what exits it.

Key themes include the relationship between backlog quality and sprint predictability, how poorly scoped stories compound into delivery drag, and why consistent output over time matters more than peak performance in any single sprint.

The practical framing centers on building delivery habits that sustain momentum — clear acceptance criteria, proactive dependency management, and retrospectives that target systemic friction rather than individual performance.
