I Haven't Written a Line of Code in Months. Am I Still a Developer?

By Dirk Steele | March 14, 2026

Read on LinkedIn

The article explores a modern identity question for experienced engineers: if AI writes more of the code, what does the developer still own?

The answer presented is not about keystrokes. It is about judgment, architecture, direction, validation, integration, and accountability for shipped outcomes.

The piece traces the historical pattern of abstraction in software, from machine language to frameworks and cloud platforms, and positions AI as the next layer rather than a replacement for human expertise.

The core thesis: developers evolve with abstraction. The strongest contributors in this era will be those who can direct AI effectively, catch errors quickly, and own delivery end-to-end.

Final reflection: if lines of code are no longer the primary metric, teams must redefine value around solution quality, decision-making, and business impact.
