Refining the Backlog in the Absence of a Product Owner

By Dirk Steele

Read on LinkedIn

Product ownership gaps are more common than most delivery frameworks acknowledge. Whether through role transitions, organizational changes, or shared accountability across stakeholders, teams frequently find themselves refining a backlog without a dedicated product owner in the room.

This article addresses that gap directly — offering practical approaches for maintaining backlog quality and prioritization clarity when product ownership is distributed or temporarily absent.

Core themes include identifying who holds prioritization authority in the absence of a formal PO, how to use existing documentation and stakeholder input to drive refinement decisions, and what signals indicate a story is ready for sprint commitment without full PO sign-off.

The broader argument is that backlog health is a team responsibility, not a single role's output. Teams that build shared understanding of value and acceptance criteria are more resilient to ownership changes and better positioned to maintain delivery momentum.
