Token Information Density and the Myth of Infinite Context

By Dirk Steele | April 17, 2025

Read on LinkedIn

This article challenges a common misunderstanding in AI-assisted work: more context does not always produce better results.

Token windows can be large, but when prompts are padded with low-signal content, essential constraints and objectives get diluted. The model receives more text, but less clarity.

The central concept is token information density: maximize useful, high-priority signal per token while reducing ambiguity and noise.

Practical guidance includes structuring prompts with explicit outcomes, critical constraints, and scoped examples. This supports stronger reasoning, fewer hallucinations, and more predictable execution.

The conclusion reframes prompt design as systems design: precision beats volume, and quality context outperforms sheer quantity.
