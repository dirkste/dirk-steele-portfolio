Anatomy of Well Written Defects

By Dirk Steele | March 26, 2025

Read on LinkedIn

Why This Matters

In software delivery, the quality of a bug report directly affects team speed, triage accuracy, and fix quality. A strong report helps others reproduce the issue, understand impact, and make the right priority decision quickly.

This article summarizes practical guidance drawn from field experience plus ideas from formal standards such as IEEE and ISO.

Quick History

Where the term bug came from

The term originated in hardware when an actual moth was found in a relay. Over time, software teams adopted the same language for defects in behavior.

Core Components

The minimum structure for high-quality defect reports

Defect ID

Use a unique identifier so issues can be tracked across current and historical work.

Title

Write concise, meaningful titles that let experienced readers understand the issue quickly.

Description

Provide focused detail without repeating what appears in repro steps and behavior sections.

Repro Steps

Document exact, repeatable steps so teammates can verify, fix, and retest without guesswork.

Observed Behavior

Describe what currently happens when steps are followed and the issue is present.

Expected Behavior

State the intended outcome so everyone aligns on what correct looks like.

Ancillary Components

Context that improves triage and fix quality

  • Related requirements

    Connect expected behavior to documented requirements.

  • Related deployments

    Call out version or release context where the issue appears.

  • Related test cases

    Reference the test case that surfaced the issue.

  • Related automation

    Link automation that can reproduce or validate the fix.

  • Version details

    Specify build, environment, and platform information.

  • Workarounds

    Include temporary paths that may affect urgency and business impact.
